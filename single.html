Despite my difficulties in understanding the study materials during classes, I began to catch on after two or three months.

Unfortunately, my exam results were disappointing, and I thought it was impossible for me to succeed. Despite this, I was determined to leave the hostel and join the day scholar batch. My focus shifted from the IIT's to the NIT's. I prepared for JEE MAIN and took the exam, but the results were also disappointing - my marks were very low. I then wrote the KEAM exam, and to my surprise, I achieved a rank of around 1500. I was surprised because I hadn't studied anything after JEE Mains.

After seeing my results, I decided to repeat for NIT. This time, I was admitted to the IIT batch at Rays, and things became much easier. I gained confidence that I could easily get into NIT, and after every mock test, I achieved a rank under 10. However, things did not go well after the results of the first JEE Mains exam.
